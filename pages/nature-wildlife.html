10 Best places to See Wildlife in Europe

Wildlife lovers are always on the prowl to look for the next best location to spot the fascinating wildlife. Europe is home to some of the most varied and abundant wildlife on the planet, making it a top destination for those interested in getting up close and personal with nature. There are countless ways to go about spotting wildlife in Europe, but the best way is to take an organized tour. An expert guide knows where you,ll have the best chance of finding specific animals, can spot wildlife you might otherwise miss, and will tell you all about the animals you want to see

1. Lynx in Romania

If you want to see lynx in the wild, your best chances can be found on guided wildlife tours in Romania.


2. Bears and birds in the Pyrenees

When: March to September

The Pyrenees mountains, shared by France, Andorra, and Spain, are home to brown bears and a host of bird species.


3. Polar bears in Svalbard, Norway

When: May to September

Visit the Svalbard archipelago during the summer months, as this is the right time to see polar bears.


4. Dolphins and whales in Italy

When: May to September

In the summer months, the Ligurian Sea is a haven for whales and dolphins.


5. Griffon vultures and bottlenose dolphins in Croatia

When: May to September

Croatia is simply awesome with its vast mountain ranges in the east and the west, and the wide range of habitats is perfect for supporting the biodiversity within Europe.


6. Bears in Greece

When: spring and autumn

Greece has hundreds of brown bears in its remote northern region, and the best way to encounter them is with an experienced guide.


7. Birdwatching on Lundy Island

When: spring and autumn

England’s Lundy is famous for its birdlife as well as the long strips of granite that rise out of the Bristol Channel; it is home to more than 35 different bird species.


8.Moose and wolves in Sweden

When: July and August

It is in the dense taiga forest of Sweden where wild animals such as moose, beavers, wolves, and deer roam free.


9. Whales in Spain

When: July and August

If you love whale watching, be sure to be in Spain in the summertime.


10. Amphibians and reptiles in Portugal

When:anytime

The mudflats and sandbanks of the Sado estuary in Portugal are teeming with some slimy and cold-blooded inhabitants.
